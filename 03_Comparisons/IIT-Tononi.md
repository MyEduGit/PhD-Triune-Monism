## IIT'S POSITION

### Tononi's Framework (2004, 2008, 2015)

Giulio Tononi proposes that consciousness IS integrated information, measured as Phi (Φ). Key claims:

1. **Consciousness is identical to integrated information** — a system is conscious to the degree it has Φ
2. **Five axioms** of consciousness (intrinsic existence, composition, information, integration, exclusion)
3. **Five postulates** — physical requirements for a system to have Φ
4. **Phi (Φ)** — a mathematical measure of how much information is generated by a system above and beyond its parts
5. **Panpsychism** — IIT entails that any system with Φ > 0 has some degree of consciousness (including thermostats, photodiodes)
6. **Exclusion postulate** — at any given time, only ONE level of integration is conscious (the one with maximum Φ)

### IIT's Approach to the Hard Problem

IIT claims to DISSOLVE the hard problem by identifying consciousness with integrated information. There is no gap because consciousness IS Φ. The axioms are derived from the immediate certainty of experience itself, and the postulates translate these into physical requirements.

## THE CORE CRITIQUE: IIT CONFUSES PATTERN WITH ENERGY

This is the Foreword's most precise philosophical critique of IIT:

The Foreword distinguishes THREE constituents of reality: energy, pattern, and personality (R = E x P x Psi). IIT identifies consciousness with PATTERN (integrated information structure). But the Foreword holds that mind is a form of ENERGY — not pattern. Pattern organizes energy but is not energy itself.

**Analogy:** A blueprint (pattern) organizes a building (physical energy). But the blueprint is not the building. Similarly, integrated information (pattern) organizes mental activity (mind energy). But the pattern is not the mind itself.

**IIT's error (per the Foreword):** Confusing the STRUCTURE of mind (which is indeed informational/pattern-based) with the SUBSTANCE of mind (which is mind energy, sourced from the Infinite Spirit). A system can have high Φ — meaning high informational integration — without having mind energy flowing through it. A thermostat has pattern (integration of temperature data) but no mind energy. Therefore, a thermostat is not conscious.

## WHERE TRIUNE MONISM IMPROVES ON IIT

### 1. The Meaning Problem

IIT measures the QUANTITY of integrated information but is blind to CONTENT. Φ is the same whether a system integrates meaningful or meaningless information. But consciousness is inherently ABOUT something — it has intentionality, directedness, meaning.

**Triune monism's advantage:** Mind gravity explains the directedness of consciousness toward meaning. Consciousness is not just integrated information — it is MEANING-SEEKING energy. The teleological character of mind is ontologically grounded.

### 2. The Arbitrary Consciousness Problem

IIT implies that a sufficiently large grid of logic gates could be conscious (if Φ is high enough). Most philosophers find this deeply counterintuitive. Searle's Chinese Room argument applies: structure alone does not guarantee understanding.

**Triune monism's advantage:** Consciousness requires mind ENERGY, not just mind pattern. A logic gate grid has physical energy organized by pattern — but no mind energy flows through it. The Infinite Spirit is not the source of logic gates.

### 3. The Values Problem (Again)

IIT cannot explain why consciousness seeks beauty, truth, and goodness. Integrated information has no inherent orientation toward values.

**Triune monism's advantage:** Spirit gravity. Values are a separate domain with their own ontological ground. This is entirely outside IIT's scope.

### 4. The Unity Problem (Different from Binding)

IIT's exclusion postulate says only one level of integration is conscious at a time. But human experience involves MULTIPLE levels simultaneously — sensory awareness, conceptual thought, emotional states, self-awareness — all unified in one experience.

**Triune monism's advantage:** Personality unifies all three domains into one experiential being. The unity is not an exclusion principle but a bestowal from the Father.

## WHERE IIT IMPROVES ON TRIUNE MONISM

### 1. Mathematical Formalization

IIT provides a precise mathematical measure (Φ). Triune monism has R = E x P x Psi, but this equation is not computationally specified.

**Response:** The equation is a philosophical framework, not a computational formula. Further formalization is future work. IIT's mathematical precision is a genuine strength.

### 2. Empirical Testability

IIT makes specific empirical predictions: systems with high Φ should be conscious; perturbational complexity index (PCI) correlates with consciousness level in clinical settings. This is testable.

**Response:** The dissertation should acknowledge IIT's empirical engagement and argue that mind gravity, while not instrumentally measurable, may correlate with neural measures of meaning-processing. Chapter 5 must address this.

### 3. Neuroscientific Integration

IIT engages directly with neuroscience — posterior cortex, thalamocortical architecture, specific neural structures. Triune monism does not engage with brain architecture.

**Response:** The dissertation must bridge this gap in Chapter 5 — suggesting how mind gravity might relate to neural correlates without being reducible to them.
