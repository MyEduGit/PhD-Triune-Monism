## Research Question

Can a triune ontology — in which reality consists of three irreducible domains (matter, mind, and spirit), each with its own source and causal circuit — resolve the hard problem of consciousness, the interaction problem, and the binding problem more successfully than physicalism, property dualism, panpsychism, and Integrated Information Theory?

## Background

The hard problem of consciousness (Chalmers, 1995) remains the central challenge in philosophy of mind. Physicalism cannot explain why physical processes give rise to subjective experience. Property dualism cannot explain mind-body interaction. Panpsychism cannot explain how micro-experiences combine into unified consciousness. Integrated Information Theory confuses informational structure with experiential substance.

This project examines a novel philosophical position — **triune monism** — derived from the ontological framework presented in the Foreword of *The Urantia Book* (1955). While the text lacks academic standing, its philosophical architecture merits rigorous evaluation on its own terms: it proposes three irreducible domains (matter, mind, spirit), four causal circuits analogous to gravity, and a unifying principle (personality) that dissolves the binding problem through ontological unity rather than neural mechanism.

## Methodology

Comparative philosophical analysis using Rescher's (1973) criteria for theory evaluation: internal coherence, explanatory scope, problem-resolution capacity, heuristic fertility, and parsimony. The framework will be systematically compared with: Chalmers' naturalistic dualism, Whitehead's process philosophy, Tononi's IIT, Searle's biological naturalism, and the standard physicalist positions (functionalism, identity theory, non-reductive physicalism).

## Original Contribution

1. **A novel philosophical position** — triune monism — presented for the first time in academic philosophy
2. **The concept of "mind gravity"** — an ontological orientation of consciousness toward meaning, providing a specific mechanism for intentionality absent from existing theories
3. **The mediator hypothesis** — the third domain (spirit/mind-source) inherently spans both material and mental domains, resolving the interaction problem without psychophysical bridging laws
4. **The personality-as-unifier thesis** — a bestowed ontological capacity (not an emergent property) that binds three domains into one experiential being, dissolving the binding problem
