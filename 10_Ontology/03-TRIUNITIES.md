## Foreword Citation

The Foreword introduces the concept of triunities as functional (non-personal) associations of three of the Seven Absolutes. The distinction from trinities:

- A **trinity** is a personal union of three Deity PERSONS (Father, Son, Spirit)
- A **triunity** is a functional association of three of the Seven ABSOLUTES (which may include non-personal members like Paradise or the potentiality Absolutes)

The Foreword hints at these relationships in Section III when describing how the First Source and Center relates to each of the other six Absolutes. Paper 104 enumerates them explicitly.

---

## What Is a Triunity?

A triunity is a three-member functional association that produces a specific domain or function of reality. Think of it as a "law of nature" at the absolute level.

**Analogy:** In physics, forces arise from interactions between fundamental particles. In the Foreword's cosmology, domains of reality arise from functional associations between Absolutes. The triunities ARE the laws.

**Rules of triunities:**
1. Every triunity includes the Father (he is the "constant" — present in all)
2. Each triunity has exactly three members
3. The association is functional, not personal (it produces something, it isn't a person)
4. All triunities are eternal (they don't begin or end)

## The Seven Triunities

### Triunity 1: FATHER + SON + SPIRIT
**The Triunity of Infinite Actuality**

| Member | Contribution |
|---|---|
| Father | Personality, will, fatherhood |
| Son | Absolute spirit, spiritual gravity |
| Spirit | Absolute mind, mind gravity, action |

**Function:** This triunity is the personal core of all reality — the three personal Deities functioning together (but NOT as the Trinity, which is a PERSONAL union; this is a FUNCTIONAL association). It accounts for the totality of PERSONAL reality.

**Domain governed:** All actual personal reality — every being with personality, every spirit person, every minded creature.

**ToE role:** This is the "personal sector" of the Theory of Everything. Everything that has personality, will, spirit, or mind traces back to this triunity.

---

### Triunity 2: FATHER + SON + PARADISE
**The Triunity of Power-Pattern**

| Member | Contribution |
|---|---|
| Father | Will, purpose, origin |
| Son | Absolute spirit person |
| Paradise | Absolute physical gravity, material pattern |

**Function:** This triunity establishes the relationship between personal spirit (Son) and non-personal matter (Paradise). It is the "bridge" between the personal and the physical.

**Domain governed:** How spirit and matter relate — the architecture of the physical universe as a stage for spiritual beings.

**ToE role:** This triunity explains WHY the physical universe exists — it exists as the arena for personal (spiritual) beings. Matter is not accidental; it is PATTERNED by Paradise to serve the Father's purpose.

---

### Triunity 3: FATHER + SPIRIT + UNQUALIFIED ABSOLUTE
**The Triunity of Force-Energy Evolution**

| Member | Contribution |
|---|---|
| Father | Will, purpose, originative causation |
| Spirit | Mind, action, cosmic manipulation |
| Unqualified Absolute | Reservoir of all non-deified potential |

**Function:** This triunity governs how cosmic force EVOLVES from primordial potential into organized energy and matter. The Unqualified Absolute supplies the raw potential, the Spirit manipulates it, and the Father directs it.

**Domain governed:** Energy transmutation — from Paradise force to physical matter. The entire sequence described in [[10-ENERGY-TRANSMUTATIONS]].

**ToE role:** This is the Foreword's equivalent of a "physics sector" — it governs how energy transforms from potentiality to actuality. This triunity is the "engine" of physical creation.

**PhD relevance:** The Spirit's role here shows that MIND (the Spirit's domain) is involved in MATERIAL creation — not just passive observation. This contradicts the physicalist separation of mind from matter.

---

### Triunity 4: FATHER + SON + DEITY ABSOLUTE
**The Triunity of Spiritual Potential Actualization**

| Member | Contribution |
|---|---|
| Father | Personality bestowal, will |
| Son | Spirit gravity, spiritual attraction |
| Deity Absolute | Reservoir of all deified potential |

**Function:** This triunity governs how new spiritual realities come into existence. The Deity Absolute holds the potential, the Son provides the spiritual gravity that "draws" it into actuality, and the Father bestows personality upon it.

**Domain governed:** The creation of new persons, new spirit beings, new divine expressions. Every new personality in the cosmos is actualized through this triunity.

**ToE role:** This explains GROWTH in the spiritual domain — why there are new beings, new creatures, new personalities. The cosmos is not static; it is perpetually creating new persons.

---

### Triunity 5: FATHER + PARADISE + UNQUALIFIED ABSOLUTE
**The Triunity of Reactive Infinity**

| Member | Contribution |
|---|---|
| Father | Will, purpose |
| Paradise | Absolute physical gravity |
| Unqualified Absolute | Non-deified cosmic potential |

**Function:** This triunity governs SPACE and cosmic force. Paradise provides the gravity center, the Unqualified Absolute provides the infinite potential, and the Father directs the process.

**Domain governed:** Space itself, cosmic force fields, the physical "stage" of the universes.

**ToE role:** This is the cosmological sector — it explains why space exists, why there is a physical cosmos, why material energy pervades infinity.

**Comparison with physics:**
This triunity is the closest to what physics studies. The relationship between gravity (Paradise), space-energy potential (Unqualified Absolute), and purposive direction (Father) maps loosely onto the relationship between gravity, dark energy, and the cosmological constant.

### Triunity 6: FATHER + SPIRIT + DEITY ABSOLUTE
**The Triunity of Cosmic Mind-Deity**

| Member | Contribution |
|---|---|
| Father | Will, personality direction |
| Spirit | Cosmic mind, mind gravity |
| Deity Absolute | All deified potential |

**Function:** This triunity governs how MIND relates to DEITY — how consciousness is oriented toward divine reality. It establishes that mind is not neutral; it is inherently oriented toward deity (toward meaning, purpose, value).

**Domain governed:** The spiritual orientation of mind — why minds naturally seek truth, beauty, and goodness.

**PhD relevance (CRITICAL):** This triunity explains WHY consciousness has an inherent orientation toward meaning. In physicalism, consciousness is value-neutral — it just processes information. In the Foreword, mind is inherently deity-oriented because the Spirit (source of mind) is associated with the Deity Absolute through this triunity. Mind doesn't merely process — it SEEKS.

### Triunity 7: FATHER + DEITY ABSOLUTE + UNIVERSAL ABSOLUTE
**The Triunity of Deity Unity**

| Member | Contribution |
|---|---|
| Father | Personal will, unifying purpose |
| Deity Absolute | All deified potential |
| Universal Absolute | Compensator between deified and undeified |

**Function:** This triunity ensures that all deity potential remains UNIFIED — that the growth of new personal beings doesn't fragment or scatter deity reality.

**Domain governed:** The coherence and unity of all deity expression — past, present, and future.

**ToE role:** This is the "conservation law" of the spiritual domain. Just as physics has conservation of energy (energy is never lost, only transformed), the Foreword has conservation of deity unity (deity is never fragmented, only diversified).

## Structural Analysis: Why These Seven?

The Father appears in ALL seven triunities. He is the constant, the unifier, the thread that runs through every functional association. This means:

> **Every domain of reality is ultimately PERSONAL — because the Father (the source of personality) participates in every triunity.**

The other six Absolutes appear in various combinations:

| Absolute | Appears in Triunities | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Father | 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 | ALL (7/7) |
| Son | 1, 2, 4 | 3/7 |
| Spirit | 1, 3, 6 | 3/7 |
| Paradise | 2, 5 | 2/7 |
| Deity Absolute | 4, 6, 7 | 3/7 |
| Unqualified Absolute | 3, 5 | 2/7 |
| Universal Absolute | 7 | 1/7 |

**Pattern:** The three personal Deities and the Deity Absolute appear most frequently (3 each, plus the Father's 7). Paradise and the Unqualified Absolute appear less (2 each). The Universal Absolute appears least (1).

**Interpretation:** Personal and deity-oriented reality is MORE CONNECTED (more triunity associations) than impersonal material reality. The cosmos is structured to favor personal, meaningful, deity-oriented outcomes.

## Unresolved Questions

### Q1: Are there exactly 7 or 15 triunities?
The Foreword mentions 7. Paper 104 may enumerate more. If there are 15, the ToE has 15 fundamental laws, not 7.

### Q2: Can triunities be combined into "higher" associations?
Just as physics seeks to unify the four forces into one, can the 7 triunities be unified into a single meta-law? The Trinity of Trinities may be this unification.

### Q3: Are triunities observable?
Physics can observe the effects of fundamental forces. Can we observe the effects of triunities? Perhaps through consciousness (Triunity 6), through physical law (Triunity 5), through spiritual experience (Triunity 4)?
