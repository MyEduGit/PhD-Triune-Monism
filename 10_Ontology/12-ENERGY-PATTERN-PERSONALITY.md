## THE TRIAD AS PHILOSOPHICAL FRAMEWORK

The triad maps onto classical philosophical categories:

| Foreword Triad | Aristotle's Four Causes | Kant's Categories | Whitehead's Categories |
|---|---|---|---|
| Energy | Material cause (what it's made of) | Quantity, quality | Creativity (the ultimate) |
| Pattern | Formal cause (what form it has) | Relation, modality | Eternal objects |
| Personality | Final cause (what purpose it serves) | — (Kant has no personal category) | Subjective aim |
| [Missing?] | Efficient cause (what made it) | Causality | Efficient causation |

**Note:** The triad does NOT have an explicit "efficient cause" component. The Foreword would say the efficient cause is the TRINITY — the activating agent that triggers actualization from the potentials. But the Trinity is not a COMPONENT of individual realities; it is the CONTEXT in which reality operates.
