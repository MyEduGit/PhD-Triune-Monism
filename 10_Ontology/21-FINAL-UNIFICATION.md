## THE THREE UNRESOLVED PROBLEMS

### Problem 1: Evil and Error
The Foreword does not explain how evil, error, or sin arise in a cosmos sourced from a perfect I AM and governed by personal Deity. This is the classical **theodicy problem** — and it remains the Foreword's most significant gap.

**Possible resolution (from later Papers):**
- Evil is the inevitable result of finite free will — creatures CAN choose wrongly
- Sin is the deliberate, knowing choice of evil
- The Father permits evil because the alternative (no free will) would be worse
- The Supreme "digests" evil — it becomes experiential wisdom

**PhD treatment:** Chapter 7 must address this honestly. The dissertation acknowledges the gap and points to later Papers for resolution, while arguing that the THREE-DOMAIN framework is still valid even if the THEODICY is incomplete.

### Problem 2: Suffering
Related to evil but distinct. Even if evil is explained by free will, why does INNOCENT suffering exist? The Foreword doesn't address this.

### Problem 3: The Mechanism of Personality Bestowal
The Father bestows personality — but HOW? What is the mechanism? The Foreword calls this a mystery, and the dissertation must respect that.
