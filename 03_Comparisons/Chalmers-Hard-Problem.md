## CHALMERS' POSITION

### The Hard Problem (1995)

David Chalmers distinguished between "easy problems" and "the hard problem":

**Easy problems** (solvable in principle by neuroscience):
- How does the brain process environmental stimulation?
- How does the brain integrate information?
- How can a cognitive system verbalize its internal states?
- How does attention work?

**The hard problem** (not solvable by neuroscience alone):
- Why is there SOMETHING IT IS LIKE to be conscious?
- Why does subjective experience exist AT ALL?
- Why do certain physical processes give rise to an inner life?

### Chalmers' Argument Against Physicalism

1. Conceivability argument: We can conceive of a "zombie" — a being physically identical to a human but with no inner experience. If zombies are conceivable, then consciousness is not logically entailed by physical properties.
2. Knowledge argument (Jackson's Mary): A color scientist who has never seen color gains NEW KNOWLEDGE when she first sees red — knowledge that was not contained in her complete physical description.
3. Explanatory gap (Levine): Even a complete physical description of the brain leaves a GAP — it doesn't explain why those physical processes feel like something.

### Chalmers' Proposed Solution: Naturalistic Dualism / Property Dualism

Chalmers proposes that consciousness is a FUNDAMENTAL FEATURE of reality — not derived from physical properties but accompanying them. He suggests psychophysical laws that connect physical states to experiential states, analogous to how fundamental physical laws connect physical quantities.

Later, Chalmers has expressed sympathy for panpsychism — the view that consciousness (or proto-consciousness) is a fundamental property of all matter.

## WHERE TRIUNE MONISM IMPROVES ON CHALMERS

### 1. The Interaction Problem

Chalmers' property dualism faces the same interaction problem as Cartesian dualism (just in a different form): if experiential properties are not physical, how do they causally interact with physical properties?

**Triune monism's advantage:** The Infinite Spirit inherently operates in BOTH domains. Mind is not "stuck" in a non-physical realm needing a bridge to the physical — the Spirit IS the bridge.

### 2. The Combination Problem

If Chalmers goes panpsychist (which he has considered), he faces the combination problem: how do micro-experiences in individual particles combine into macro-experience in brains?

**Triune monism's advantage:** There is no combination problem because mind is not a property of individual particles. Mind is a separate DOMAIN with its own gravity circuit. Macro-experience arises from mind gravity unifying mental activity, not from combining particle-level proto-experiences.

### 3. Why Consciousness Seeks Meaning

Chalmers does not explain why consciousness has a teleological character — why it seeks truth, beauty, goodness, meaning. His framework describes consciousness but does not explain its orientation.

**Triune monism's advantage:** Triunity 6 (Father + Spirit + Deity Absolute) establishes that mind is inherently oriented toward deity reality. Mind gravity draws consciousness toward meaning. This is a specific, novel explanation.

### 4. The Unity of Consciousness

Chalmers acknowledges the binding problem but does not fully resolve it. Why is experience UNIFIED rather than fragmented?

**Triune monism's advantage:** Personality (bestowed by the Father) is the unifying principle. It makes the three domains into ONE experiential being. The binding problem is solved ontologically, not neurologically.

## WHERE CHALMERS IMPROVES ON TRIUNE MONISM

### 1. Parsimony
Chalmers' property dualism adds ONE extra thing to physics (experiential properties). Triune monism adds TWO extra domains (mind and spirit) plus personality, gravity circuits, and the I AM. Chalmers is more parsimonious.

**Response:** Parsimony is only relevant when explanatory power is equal. Triune monism explains MORE (interaction, binding, meaning-seeking, values) — the extra commitments are justified.

### 2. Engagement with Neuroscience
Chalmers engages directly with neuroscientific data (neural correlates of consciousness, IIT, Global Workspace Theory). Triune monism does not engage with empirical neuroscience.

**Response:** The dissertation must engage with neuroscience in Chapter 5 — showing how mind gravity might relate to neural correlates, not replace them.

### 3. Academic Standing
Chalmers is the most cited philosopher of mind alive. Triune monism is derived from a text with no academic standing.

**Response:** Arguments are evaluated on their merits, not their pedigree. But the dissertation must be especially rigorous to overcome this disadvantage.
