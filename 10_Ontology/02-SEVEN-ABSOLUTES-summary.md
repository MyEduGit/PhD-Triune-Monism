## The Seven Absolutes at a Glance

| # | Absolute | Nature | Domain | Gravity Circuit |
|---|----------|--------|--------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Universal Father** | Personal, existential | Personality, will | Personality gravity |
| 2 | **Eternal Son** | Personal, existential | Spirit, spiritual values | Spirit gravity |
| 3 | **Infinite Spirit** | Personal, existential | Mind, action, mediation | Mind gravity |
| 4 | **Isle of Paradise** | Non-personal, existential | Physical gravity, pattern | Physical gravity |
| 5 | **Deity Absolute** | Non-personal, potential | All deified potential | None (reservoir) |
| 6 | **Unqualified Absolute** | Non-personal, potential | All undeified potential | None (reservoir) |
| 7 | **Universal Absolute** | Non-personal, compensating | Unity of 5 and 6 | None (balancer) |

## Structural Logic: Why 3 + 1 + 3 = 7

- **3 Personal Deities:** Will (Father) + Expression (Son) + Action (Spirit) — exhausts personal categories
- **1 Non-Personal Center:** Paradise — the singular pattern for all material reality
- **3 Potentiality Absolutes:** Deified potential + Undeified potential + Unifier — exhausts potential categories

## Four Gravity Circuits (the "forces" of the ToE)

1. **Physical gravity** — Paradise pulls all matter
2. **Spirit gravity** — Eternal Son pulls all spirit
3. **Mind gravity** — Infinite Spirit pulls all consciousness
4. **Personality gravity** — Father pulls all personalities

Physics knows one gravity. The Foreword claims four.

## PhD Key Insight
The Infinite Spirit solves the mind-body problem: it is simultaneously the source of mind AND the manipulator of matter. Mind interacts with matter because the Spirit operates across BOTH domains.
