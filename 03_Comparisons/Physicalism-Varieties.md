**Priority:** ESSENTIAL — Physicalism is the dominant position in philosophy of mind

## PHYSICALISM'S POSITION

Physicalism holds that everything that exists is physical, or supervenes on the physical. Varieties:

### 1. Identity Theory (Smart, Place)
Conscious states ARE brain states. Pain = C-fiber firing. Type-type identity.

**Problem:** Multiple realizability (Putnam). Pain can occur in different physical substrates (octopus nervous systems, hypothetical silicon brains). Therefore mental types cannot be identical to physical types.

### 2. Functionalism (Putnam, Fodor, early)
Mental states are defined by their FUNCTIONAL ROLES — causal relations to inputs, outputs, and other mental states. What matters is the function, not the physical substrate.

**Problem:** Absent qualia (Block). A system could have the right functional organization but no subjective experience. China Brain thought experiment — 1.4 billion people simulating neural connections could be functionally equivalent to a brain, but would the nation be conscious?

### 3. Eliminativism (Churchlands)
Folk psychology (beliefs, desires, consciousness) is a FALSE theory that will be eliminated by neuroscience. There is no "consciousness" — just neural processes we mislabel.

**Problem:** Self-refuting. The claim "consciousness does not exist" is itself a conscious assertion. To deny experience while having experience is incoherent.

### 4. Reductive Physicalism
Consciousness can be FULLY explained by, and reduced to, neural processes. We just need more neuroscience.

**Problem:** The explanatory gap (Levine). Even a complete neural description leaves unexplained WHY those processes feel like something. Knowing all the physics does not tell you what red LOOKS LIKE.

### 5. Non-Reductive Physicalism (Davidson, Kim)
Mental properties are real but SUPERVENE on physical properties. No physical difference without a mental difference. Mental events are token-identical to physical events but not type-identical.

**Problem:** Causal exclusion (Kim). If every physical event has a sufficient physical cause, then mental events are causally redundant. Either the mental IS the physical (collapse into identity theory) or the mental does nothing (epiphenomenalism).

### 6. Illusionism (Frankish)
Consciousness exists but phenomenal properties (qualia, "what-it-is-like-ness") are an ILLUSION. There is something going on, but it's not what we think.

**Problem:** What generates the illusion? Illusion IS a conscious experience. You cannot explain away consciousness by invoking consciousness.

## WHY ALL PHYSICALISM FAILS (THE PhD'S CORE ARGUMENT)

The Foreword's framework identifies the STRUCTURAL reason all physicalist approaches fail:

**They confuse domains.** All physicalist theories attempt to explain mind-domain phenomena (meanings, qualia, intentionality) using matter-domain categories (neurons, functions, information processing, physical laws). This is a CATEGORY ERROR — like trying to explain why a joke is funny using only the physics of sound waves.

The three-domain framework predicts that this category error is irremediable — not because we lack knowledge, but because mind is genuinely NOT physical. No amount of physical description will ever capture what-it-is-like-ness, because what-it-is-like-ness belongs to a different domain with a different source (Infinite Spirit) and a different gravity circuit (mind gravity).

## THE FOREWORD'S SPECIFIC RESPONSES

| Physicalist Claim | Foreword Response |
|---|---|
| "Consciousness is brain activity" | Brain activity is MATTER domain. Consciousness is MIND domain. They correlate (via Spirit's mediation) but are not identical. |
| "Consciousness is functional organization" | Function is PATTERN, not mind energy. Pattern organizes mind energy but is not mind itself. (Same critique as IIT.) |
| "Consciousness will be eliminated by neuroscience" | Mind is a separate domain with its own source. Neuroscience studies the matter-domain correlates of mind, not mind itself. |
| "Consciousness supervenes on the physical" | Mind has its own gravity circuit independent of physical gravity. Supervenience confuses correlation with dependence. |
| "Consciousness is an illusion" | Illusion requires a subject who is deceived — which presupposes consciousness. Self-refuting. |
| "We just need more neuroscience" | More neuroscience = more matter-domain knowledge. The mind domain requires its own investigation. |
